About me

I'm a graduate student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Texas at Austin, where I'm a Graduate Research Assistant in the Wang Lab. My research focuses on building personalized automatic speech recognition systems for individuals with disordered speech — including stuttering, dysarthria, and apraxia — with adaptive pipelines that improve over time for each user.

I completed my B.S. in ECE at Northeastern University, where I worked in the Action Lab on human-robot collaboration and ran engineering workshops through the Sherman Center. Before graduate school, I spent two years as an Electronic & Software Integration Technician at Vigilant Biosciences, building sensor systems and software tools for FDA-regulated medical devices.

I'm interested in the intersection of AI, signal processing, and accessibility — building systems that work reliably for people who are often underserved by off-the-shelf technology.

Projects

VitalLink — Wearable Triage System

Capstone · Northeastern University · 2024–2025  ·  GitHub

BLE-enabled wristband and base-station pipeline for continuous patient vitals monitoring (heart rate, SpO2, temperature, motion) in hospital waiting rooms. Generates real-time severity scores to help clinicians prioritize care. Designed custom PCB hardware and firmware; built the clinical-facing web dashboard.

CaptionXR — Real-Time XR Captioning

Personal project  ·  GitHub

Unity-based live caption overlay system that renders real-time speech captions within an XR environment, integrating speech recognition with spatial UI rendering for accessibility in extended reality.
